# Privacy Policy

Last updated: June 19, 2026

Simple row counter is available on iOS and Android. It does not run its own server and does not
sell or share personal data. Your projects stay on your device unless you turn on optional cloud
sync, which uses your own iCloud account (on iOS) or Google Drive account (on Android).

## Data Stored By The App

The app stores row counters, project names, linked counters, goals, and local app settings on
the user's device. If the user imports a PDF pattern, that file is also kept on the device, in
the app's private storage.

If the user enables cloud sync, the app stores a sync snapshot in the user's own cloud account:

- On iOS, in the user's private iCloud (CloudKit) storage, tied to the Apple ID signed in on the
  device.
- On Android, in the user's Google Drive app data folder. This folder is private to the app and
  is not visible in the user's normal Drive files. The app also stores the selected Google
  account email on the device so it can show which account is connected.

In both cases the data stays in the user's own cloud account. The developer has no server and
cannot access it.

## Cloud Account Access

Cloud sync is optional and off by default.

On iOS, sync uses the iCloud account already signed in on the device. The app never asks for an
email or a password; Apple manages access through the system.

On Android, the app asks for the Google Drive app data permission only when the user turns sync
on. It uses this permission only to read, create, update, and delete its own sync data in the app
data folder, and does not access the user's visible Drive files. The app does not store Google
passwords; Google Play Services manages the authorization tokens.

## Payments

If the user subscribes, billing is handled by the App Store on iOS or Google Play on Android. The
app does not receive or store payment card details.

## Data Sharing And Tracking

Simple row counter does not sell or share user data with third parties. The app does not use ads,
analytics, or tracking software.

## Security

Local app data is stored in the operating system's app-private storage. Network requests — to
iCloud and the App Store on iOS, to Google Drive and Google Play on Android, and to the website
host (GitHub Pages and Cloudflare) — use HTTPS.

## Data Deletion

Users can delete local app data by deleting projects in the app or uninstalling the app.

Turning sync off stops further syncing but leaves the existing snapshot in the cloud:

- On iOS, open the iOS Settings, tap your name, then iCloud, manage the app's storage, and delete
  its data.
- On Android, remove the app's access from your Google Account settings, which erases the app data
  folder.
